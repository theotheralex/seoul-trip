Dinner · The Busan experience

Jagalchi Market — pick your seafood live

Korea’s largest seafood market. Pick live fish, crab, octopus from vendors. Take it upstairs and have it cooked for you. As fresh as it gets.

SeafoodIconic Busan
Details

Ground floor: vendors with live fish, crab, octopus, sea urchin, abalone in tanks — pick what you want. Upstairs: restaurants cook your selection. Stall 183 vendor is famously English-friendly and generous with extras like sea urchin and scallops.

Beitou Hot Springs

Geothermal spring district 30 min by MRT. Public outdoor pools or private room soaking. Misty mountain setting. Perfect November weather for it.

Hot springsRelaxation
Details

Beitou (北投) is a hot spring resort district north of Taipei — take the MRT Red Line to Xinbeitou Station (~30 min from Ximen). Geothermal spring water ranges from sulfuric milky white to clear.

Options: Millennium Hot Spring (public outdoor pools, NT$40 — seriously cheap), or rent a private room at one of the many hot spring hotels for a 2-hour soak (NT$500–1,500/room for 4 people). November weather makes outdoor soaking especially good — crisp air, hot water.

Dinner · The Taiwan meal

Din Tai Fung — The original xiao long bao

Where soup dumplings became a global obsession. The original Taiwan location. Order the classics: xiao long bao, shrimp fried rice, cucumber salad. Expect a wait — book ahead or pre-order.

MichelinMust eat
Details

Din Tai Fung (鼎泰豐) started in Taipei and the original locations here are the gold standard. The B1 branch inside Taipei 101 is the most convenient after the 101 visit, or the Xinyi Anhe branch is nearby and less chaotic.

Order: xiao long bao (pork soup dumplings — minimum 2 steamers), shrimp and pork shao mai, shrimp fried rice, cucumber in garlic, red bean dumplings for dessert. For a group of 4, budget NT$800–1,200/person. Pre-order on Klook to reduce wait time.
