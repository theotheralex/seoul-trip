Evening · First meal in Korea

Korean BBQ — Night 1 tradition

Grill pork belly tableside, wrap in ssam, chase with kimchi jjigae and soju. The only acceptable first meal in Seoul.

KBBQNight 1
More info

Walk out of the Airbnb and head down Hongik-ro — there are KBBQ spots everywhere. Look for places with good ventilation (you'll see the vent hoods over each table). Order: samgyeopsal (pork belly), chadolbaegi (thin beef), corn cheese, and steamed egg. Soju is mandatory. Cost: roughly ₩15,000–25,000 per person.

Gold Pig (Geumdo-nae) is the premium version with Jeju pork if you want to go all-in, but an honest local spot in Hongdae will be just as good on night 1.

Late night · Group activity

Noraebang — Private karaoke room

Private room for the 4 of you. Pick songs, order drinks delivered, scream lyrics. Seoul's best group tradition.

Group activityRoom culture
More info

Noraebang is Korean private-room karaoke — totally different from bar karaoke. You get a private room with a giant screen, microphones, tambourines, and a tablet to pick songs. Huge English song library. Rooms are rented by the hour: ~₩15,000–20,000/hr for a group room.

Walk Hongik-ro and look for neon microphone signs. The ones with glass windows on the street (so passersby can watch you) are especially fun. Coin noraebang is also available for 30-min micro-sessions.

Dinner · Seoul ritual

Chimaek — Fried chicken + beer night

Double-fried Korean fried chicken with ice-cold beer. A Seoul institution. Perfect group meal after a day of cards.

Group mealSeoul ritual
More info

Chimaek = chicken + maekju (beer). Korean fried chicken is double-fried for extra crunch, then tossed in soy-garlic, spicy, or honey-butter sauce. Order multiple styles. Kkanbu Chicken in Hongdae/Sinchon is a go-to local spot. Cost: ~₩30,000–40,000 for a full spread for 4.

This works as a big table meal — everyone orders different sauces and shares. Pairs naturally with cold draft beer or soju.

Late night

Arcade hopping in Hongdae

Coin-op arcades with rhythm games (DDR, Pump It Up), UFO catchers, and retro cabinets. Open late.

ArcadeLate night
More info

Hongdae has multiple coin-op arcades clustered along the main shopping street. Look for the ones with rhythm game setups (DDR and Pump It Up are everywhere). There are also crane/UFO catcher machines with anime and game merch as prizes. Coin exchange at the counter — each game is ₩500–₩1,000.

No cover charge, just buy coins. Arcades in Hongdae stay open past midnight on weekends.

Anime figuresGeek HQ

04

Palaces, Pancakes + PC Bang

Thursday · October 30

Best fall foliage day at Changdeokgung's Secret Garden, then the best street food market in Seoul for lunch, then a PC bang session in the afternoon — playing games together in Korea's cathedral of PC gaming. Finish with the best beef tartare in the city.

Morning · Book tickets in advance

Changdeokgung + Secret Garden

The most beautiful palace in Seoul. Secret Garden has limited access — book ahead. Fall foliage in late October is genuinely stunning here.

CultureBest in fall
More info

Changdeokgung Palace (창덕궁) is considered the most beautiful of Seoul's royal palaces — smaller than Gyeongbokgung but more atmospheric. The Secret Garden (후원) is the highlight: a secluded 78-acre garden with pavilions, ponds, and ancient trees. Guided tours are required for the garden and slots sell out, so book in advance on the official site.

Late October is peak fall foliage season — the maple and ginkgo trees around the garden turn brilliant red and gold. After the palace, walk 10 min to Bukchon Hanok Village for photos among traditional Korean houses.

↗ Book Secret Garden tickets (official) ↗ Changdeokgung on Maps

Lunch · Market grazing

Gwangjang Market

Seoul's oldest market. Mung bean pancakes, yukhoe (beef tartare), handmade noodles, makgeolli. As seen on Netflix Street Food: Asia.

Street foodMust do
More info

Gwangjang Market (광장시장) has been open for over a century. The street food section is the main draw — walk through and graze. Must try: bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes, crispy and savory), mayak gimbap (tiny addictive seaweed rice rolls), and kalguksu handmade knife-cut noodles at Gohyang Sonkalguksu (the Netflix-famous stall).

Also try yukhoe (raw beef tartare) with pear and egg — if you had Buchon Yukhoe on the list, this is a preview. Wash everything down with makgeolli (rice wine, served in bowls). Budget: ₩15,000–25,000/person for a proper market lunch.

↗ Gwangjang Market on Maps

Afternoon · Squad gaming session

PC Bang — Korean gaming cafe

Ultra-fast PCs, free snacks and drinks, queue up online together. The Gen.G GGX in Dongdaemun is the premium new-gen version.

PC BangCultural experience
More info

PC bangs are a Korean institution — high-spec gaming PCs, fast internet, and a food/drinks menu you order from your seat. Every major game is installed. This is where Korean esports culture was born. Gen.G Gaming Experience (GGX) in Dongdaemun is a newly-opened premium facility — the next-gen PC bang designed as a lifestyle hub, not just a gaming cafe.

Cost: ₩1,500–₩3,000/hr per person. Budget 2–3 hours. Load up some League, Valorant, or whatever the group wants to grind together while sitting next to each other IRL.

↗ PC Bangs near Dongdaemun

Dinner · Michelin-recognized

Buchon Yukhoe — 9-time Michelin pick

The best beef tartare in Seoul. Raw beef, egg yolk, crunchy Asian pear. Michelin-recognized 9 consecutive years. Get there early.

MichelinDon't miss
More info

Buchon Yukhoe (북촌육회) has been Michelin-recognized for nine consecutive years. The dish: fresh raw beef mixed with julienned Asian pear and pine nuts, topped with a runny egg yolk, finished with sesame oil and a drizzle of soy. It's unlike anything you've had. It's recommended to share one plate between 2–3 people as a starter.

Small restaurant, fills up fast. Arrive when it opens or expect a wait. Located near Bukchon Hanok Village.

LoL Park + Halloween Hongdae

Friday · October 31 · Halloween

You are in Hongdae on Halloween. This is incredibly lucky. 100,000+ people in elaborate costumes flood the streets — it's one of the wildest Halloween parties in Asia. LoL Park in the afternoon, then come back and let Hongdae take over the night.

Afternoon · Esports pilgrimage

LoL Park (CHZZK LoL Park)

Home of Korea's LCK — the world's best League of Legends league. Riot Store, PC bang inside, exclusive merch. Check if a match is on Oct 28–31.

Esports meccaGamer pilgrimage
More info

Address: 33 Jong-ro, Jongno-gu, Seoul — 3rd floor of the Gran Seoul Building (Jonggak Station, Exit 1). This is the home of the LCK, Korea's top professional League of Legends league. Korean esports teams have won more World Championships than any other region.

Even without a live match: the Riot Store stocks exclusive LoL Park-only merch (better selection than the T1 shop in Hongdae), there's a full high-spec PC bang inside, and the entire space is a love letter to the game. If a match is scheduled during your stay (Oct 28–31), buy tickets immediately on Interpark — they sell out fast. Weekday tickets: ₩41,000. Weekend: ₩51,000.

Night · Halloween in Hongdae

Halloween street party — 100,000+ people

Hongdae goes completely wild on Oct 31. Costumes, street food, bars, noraebang. One of the best Halloween parties in Asia. No ticket needed.

HalloweenLucky timing

Halloween in Hongdae is a massive deal — in 2025, over 110,000 people showed up in elaborate costumes. The entire neighborhood becomes a free outdoor festival.
